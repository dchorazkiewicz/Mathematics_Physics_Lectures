# 5. Current Direction vs Electron Motion

## Goal

Show that conventional current and electron motion have opposite directions in metals.

## Slide layout

Title:

**Current Direction vs Electron Motion**

Center: horizontal conductor connected to a battery.

Place the positive terminal on the left and the negative terminal on the right.

Above the conductor, draw a thick red/orange arrow to the right:

**conventional current (I)**

Inside or below the conductor, draw blue electron arrows to the left:

**electron motion**

Bottom caption:

**The current direction is a convention**

## Visual details

Electrons are blue dots with (-). Current arrow is red/orange. Battery terminals clearly marked (+) and (-).

## Avoid

Do not overexplain the historical reason. The image should make the opposition obvious.
