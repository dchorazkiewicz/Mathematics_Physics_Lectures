# 14. Why Does a Wire Heat Up?

## Goal

Show that collisions transfer energy from moving electrons to the lattice.

## Slide layout

Title:

**Why Does a Wire Heat Up?**

Center: conductor carrying current.

Left side: electrons enter with directed motion.

Middle: collisions with lattice ions.

Right side: conductor becomes warmer, with red/orange glow.

Use arrows:

**electrical energy**

to

**thermal energy**

Bottom:

**Collisions transfer energy to the lattice**

## Visual details

Draw positive ions vibrating more strongly after collisions. Add small red wavy heat lines. Current arrow should still be visible.

## Avoid

Do not make it look like the electrons themselves are burning. Heat belongs to the lattice/material.
