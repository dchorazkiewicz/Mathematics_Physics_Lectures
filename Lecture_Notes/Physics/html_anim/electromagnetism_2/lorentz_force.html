Lorentz Force: motion of a charged particle in a uniform magnetic field

The magnetic field is perpendicular to the screen. Positive B means the field points out of the screen, negative B means it points into the screen. The particle moves in the plane, while the Lorentz force keeps turning the velocity vector.

In this model the magnetic force is always perpendicular to the instantaneous velocity, so it changes the direction of motion but not the speed. That is why a pure magnetic field does no work on the particle.
