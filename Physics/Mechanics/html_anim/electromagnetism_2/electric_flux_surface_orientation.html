Electric Flux Through a Flat Surface

This version uses a real 3D perspective view. The same rectangular surface is shown in three spatial orientations relative to a uniform electric field E, and the middle panel rotates continuously. Electric flux still follows Φ = EA cos φ, but now the geometry is visible as a true 3D tilt of the plane and its area vector.

The same surface area can intercept different amounts of field depending on orientation. The relevant quantity is the component of the area vector along the field direction, so the effective area is A⊥ = A cos φ. Drag on the canvas to rotate the 3D camera.
